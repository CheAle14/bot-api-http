As a Justice, you are part of a panel of Justices that are selected to represent the Court of Appeals
You are expected to deliberate with the other Justices, then finally reach a verdict
This verdict may typically involve:

  • Unanimous - all Justices agree entirely on a way forward
  • Majority - most Justices agree, or concur
  • Concur - reaching the same conclusion, but with some (or most) different reasons
  • Minority - the Justices that disagree with the majority verdict
Any descision must have a majority behind it; that means at least justices must all concur

Below are the possible options this Court may come to:

  • Dismiss - for cases incorrect on a procedural point (eg, plaintiff having no standing, Court having no jurisdiction)
  • Uphold - The Tournament Moderator's descision was entirely correct and requires no correction
  • Remand - Make a requirement of some sort (eg, a limit) then send the matter back down to the Moderator for them to take those requirements into account
  • Overturn - Remove the Moderator's descision entirely and the Court issues its own
  • Other - for when the other verdicts dont fit (you must provide proper explaination)

Please note: You are expected to be able to explain your position, such that it can be written up and released
