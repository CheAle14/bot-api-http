Preamble

RECOGNISING that the Leaderboard could be misused or abused;
RECOGNISING that players may attempt to cheat in games;
NOTING the need for some system to reverse such misuse, and penalise abuse and cheating;
NOTING that such a system cannot be actioned by one person, and should have proper appeals cases
HENCEFORTH DECLARING: The creation of Tournament Moderators to ensure a free and fair set of games; The creation of the Court of Appeals (CoA) which consists of Justices and a Chief Justice, who shall hear any matter pertaining to the function of this Leaderboard, specifically any appeal on the penalties given by Moderators

    Jurisdiction of these Terms and the Court of Appeals

  1. The Long Title for these Terms is: Friday Chess Club Leaderboard Terms, Conditions and Rules
  2. The Short Title for these Terms is: Chess Terms and Conditions
  3. These Terms apply to any game that involves the use of the Leaderboard at https://ml-api.uk.ms
  4. These Terms do not apply to any private game which does not involve the Leaderboard
  5. Any games that do not take place on a Friday, between 12:30 and 13:10 in 322 are considered invalid
  6. Any games that do not take place with at least one Moderator present in the room are considered invalid
  7. Any games that take place with at least one person who is currently banned are invalid
  8. For the purpose of these Terms:
      "Person" is to refer to any natural distinct individual "Student" is to refer to a person who is enrolled at Coundon Court "Member" is to refer to any student who has joined the Leaderboard "Present" is to be defined as being within the confounds of 322 during Chess Club, for no less than 5 minutes
  9. Any games which are invalid shall not be entered into the Leaderboard

    Section I - Rules in Game

  1. This Leaderboard follows the FIDE laws of chess.
    Except in FIDE 4.2: Adjusting a piece requires no verbal communication so long as it is clear, but must be performed during your turn
    Except in relation to a chessclock, which is not required.
    Except in relation to any FIDE rule that would violate the principle of casual play, placing an unnecessary burden on players
    Except in relation to any FIDE rule that would contradict, either implicitly or explicitly, any Act in these Terms
  2. Unsportsmanlike conduct and cheating are prohibited
    1. Such conduct may be penalised through score deductions, time penalties, game forfeiture, banning or a combination of them
  3. There is no time limit imposed on games, other than that imposed by the school bell, or both players
  4. Any student is encouraged to attend, either to spectate or to play.
  5. If a game is forced to end by lack of time, and neither player resigns, then the game can be ended by any Moderator and decided as follows:
    For each player, add up the pieces of the opponent that they have took and assign them a score as so:
    Pawns - 1
    Bishops & Knights - 3
    Rooks - 5
    Queen - 9
    Whichever player has the highest score shall win
  6. If a player objects to a game's ending, then a rematch may occur on the following available session
    If the petitioner fails to play the rematch, then the original game is final
    If the opposing player fails to play the rematch, then the game played is forfeit
    A rematched game is final, a second rematch cannot be performed.
    A rematch must have a Moderator or Justice overseeing the game to adjudicate the game.
    Unless exempted by the Moderator adjudicating the game, all rematches must be time limited to 5 minutes each, with a chessclock preferred.
  7. If a chessclock is used, the following shall apply:
    1. A Moderator or Justice must be present throughout the game.
    2. Both players must approve and assent to the chessclock being used
    3. The chessclock must have two timers, one for each player, that counts down. The chessclock must have an ability to toggle which timer is currently counting down. The chessclock must also have an ability to pause and restart both timers.
    4. The chessclock should have the ability to keep track of moves, though this is not required
    5. If a player's timer reaches zero, then the game is immediately ended with their opponent winning.
    6. Any FIDE rule of chess that would apply to chessclocks and is not otherwise exempt applies.
  8. If a game is generally time-limited without use of a chessclock as defined above, the following shall apply:
    1. A Moderator or Justice must be informed of the time limit; when the game begins; any pauses throughout the game; when the game should end.
    2. If the time limit expires, then the game shall be instantly ended as per Act 5.

    Section II - Leaderboard

  1. Any student is freely permitted to join the Leaderboard with an initial score of 100
  2. A score below 100 is not permitted
  3. Changes to the score shall be determined by the Elo Rating system, as implemented; changes may also be given by Moderators, subject to overruling or affirmation
  4. Members will be expected to register games played
    1. Addendum: Members must only register games that were actually played. Any registration of a fictitious game is considered fraudulent as per below
  5. Attempting to fraudulently gain points will result in point deductions being applied
    On any first violation, the action must be reversed and points returned as though they never happened
    On any second violation, the player's points are to be reduced by half
    On any third violation, the player's points are to be reset to 100
    Any fourth violation results in an immediate four week ban
    Any fifth violation results in a permanent ban
    Moderators may use discretion in the application of the above
  6. No show policy: Members that are not present for three consecutive weeks will have their score reduced by 40 for each week until reset to 100
  7. Any Member that has not been present for more than three of the past 6 weeks, and who has played no more than two games within the same frame may be removed from the Leaderboard by the Chief Justice unilaterally
    After, the Student shall be entered into the Leaderboard as per Act 1 and 2 of Section II
    This Act may be used on any banned Member should the conditions be met. Under these circumstances, the Student is forbidden to rejoin until the ban is revoked
  8. Any Member may request directly to the Chief Justice that they be removed from the Leaderboard; the Chief Justice must comply with this request within seven (7) days
    1. If the Member is banned, or there is currently a Court hearing involving them, then the Chief Justice must not comply until such circumstance has passed
  9. A Member may be banned from the Leaderboard by any independent Moderator for repeated violations, or for a serious misconduct, or for actions that bring this Leaderboard or her players into disrepute
    1. Bans must have a valid reason
    2. Bans must have an expiry date, which must fall on a Friday
    3. Bans can be petitioned to the Court, which may elect to shorten or lengthen the ban, or remove it in its entirety
    4. Only the Court of Appeals may remove or shorten, or lengthen a ban
    5. After one appeal, the Court may refuse to hear any further Appeals for specified finite duration
  10. A Member may set to monitored by any Moderator
    1. Monitors can only be placed if the Moderator has reasonable suspicion that a violation of the Terms had occurred, or could occur
    2. Monitor may be removed by any Moderator if there is no longer reasonable suspicion to support it being in place
    3. When monitored, any games played by a Member must be overseen (either partially, or in entirety) by a Moderator
    4. When monitored, any game added involving the Member must be approved as valid by the previous, or any, Moderator
    5. If a monitor is not removed after the session immediately following it being put in place, the Member may petition the Court in an appeal against the Moderator to remove it immediately.
      Under these circumstances, the Moderator must give forth their reasonable suspicion, and why it still applies
    6. The Member may petition the Court that the Moderator's suspicion is not reasonable, and thus the Monitor is not permissible under these Terms
  11. Under any circumstances where the above Acts indicate action must, shall, will or is to be taken with no alternative or discretion, the Chief Justice may unilaterally take action in accordance to those Acts.
    1. Any such action should be documented as soon as possible
    2. If the actions taken are not supported by Acts in these Terms, the Court of Appeals may hear the petition

    Section III - Administration

  1. The Leaderboard is Hosted and Administered by Alex C
  2. Tournament Moderators are appointed by the Chief Justice to immediately resolve issues
    1. The current Moderators are:
    2. Whilst Moderators have broad authority to take steps to ensure the integrity of the Leaderboard, the following shall not be infringed:
        Each Member's right to due process: any decision must be reasonably justifiable Each Member's right to enjoyment: any decision must not unnecessarily harm any member Each Member's right to equality: any decisions issued must be free of unreasonable bias Each Member's right against double jeopardy: no penalty shall twice be issued for the same offence, when a previous penalty had been issued
    3. A person shall only be severely sanctioned if it can be proven they acted either with intent and knowledge, or through negligence
      1. For the purposes of this Act, a person has "intent" where:
        a) In relation to conduct, that person means to engage in the conduct;
        b) In relation to a consequence, that person means to cause that consequence or is aware that it will occur in the ordinary course of events.
      2. For the purposes of this Act, a person shall be negligent where:
        a) In relation to conduct, the person engaged in the conduct, either with partial or entire intent to do so;
        b) In relation to a consequence, any average person would know that it would occur in the ordinary course of events
    4. Within seven (7) days of an individual exercising Moderator powers, such individual must publicly declare what action was taken, who it was against, and the reasoning for it
      1. For the purposes of this Act, a declaration is public if either:
        a) It was sent via email to all the Moderators, Justices, Chief Justice and the accused
        b) It was sent via Discord, in the #discussion channel.
      2. Failure to provide this declaration shall automatically void any penalty given
      3. Failure to provide proper reasoning either in this declaration or after, as determined under appeal, may cause grounds to invalidate the penalty, either in severity, or in entirety
  3. The Court of Appeals (CoA) hears matters on appeal from resolutions issued by the aforementioned moderators, or any other matters concerning the Leaderboard
    1. The staff of the CoA currently are:
        Chief Justice Alex C
    2. The Court of Appeals should hear cases in panels of three justices, with a majority for a judgement; except for when the Chief Justice is exercising discretionary powers: then they act in Solo
    3. Justices may exercise Moderator power if needed.
    4. Moderators shall not act in games they took part of; Justices shall not hear cases they have a conflict in
      1. If there are not enough Justices to be empanelled because of a conflict, then their conflict may be ignored and them selected anyway
      2. Conflict only applies to the Chief Justice in order to prevent them from sitting on a panel for a case.
      3. Conflict does not apply to an en banc hearing - every Justice must hear the case.
    5. Whether the Court of Appeals hears a case is granted either by the Chief Justice, or at least independent two justices
    6. The panel of justices to hear a case is selected by the Chief Justice
    7. The panel may make one of three decisions:
      a) Remanding the decision back down to the Moderator, requiring that they re-evaluate with conditions
      b) Overturn and making their own decision in its place
      c) Upholding the original decision
    8. A judgement from a panel can be appealed to be heard en banc, by the whole Court.
      1. Whether the Court accepts to hear the appeal is up to the Chief Justice
      2. This would be the final appeal, and would be binding
    9. Within seven (7) days of the Court reaching a verdict, the Court must present its majority opinion in the form of a written document explaining the reasoning as to why the Court came to its decision. The Chief Justice may provide formatting assistance, and, if they are in the majority, may choose who shall write up the Court's verdict.
  4. Any authority not explicitly granted may be exercised unilaterally by the Chief Justice
  5. The Chief Justice may make technical modifications to the Leaderboard if reasonably necessary, subject to challenge by an en banc hearing
    1. Any technical modification must be publicly announced

    Section IV - Modification to these Terms

  1. These Terms may be unilaterally changed by the Chief Justice at any times
  2. It is the responsibility of each Member to keep up-to-date with the Terms, ignorance of these Terms is not considered a valid defence
  3. Members may suggest changes to the Terms if they desire
  4. Moderators and Justices are entitled to submit pull requests to these Terms, though their acceptance is not guaranteed
